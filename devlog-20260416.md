## Work done

### 1. Parakeet-TDT LibriSpeech run

Launched `parakeet-tdt-1.1b` + `parakeet-tdt-0.6b-v3` on Jetson Orin 32GB
against full LibriSpeech test-clean (2618 samples) and test-other (2937).
Local `.nemo` files already on Orin (HF blocked). Ran via vibevoice-venv
with NeMo toolkit.

**Results:**

| Model | LS-clean WER | LS-other WER | RTF | VRAM |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| parakeet-tdt-1.1b | **1.82%** | **3.43%** | 0.052 | 4.17 GB |
| parakeet-tdt-0.6b-v3 | 2.56% | 4.63% | 0.038 | 2.60 GB |

**Findings:**

- Parakeet-TDT-1.1B ties Cohere Transcribe 2B on LS-clean (1.82 vs 1.80%,
  2-error gap across 2618 utterances — within noise).
- **Parakeet-TDT-1.1B is the new LS-other leader at 3.43%**, beating
  Qwen3-ASR-1.7B (4.12%) by 0.69 pp — best LS-other result in the bench.
- Parakeet-TDT-0.6B-v3 is the fastest GPU model in the bench (RTF 0.038).
- Both Parakeets are EN-only (0.6B-v3 covers 25 European languages but
  explicitly no ZH/JA) — not a single-model replacement for Bosco's
  EN/ZH/JA use case.

### 2. sugr-asr-bench top-3 EN long-form

Ran three candidates (cohere-transcribe-2b, parakeet-tdt-1.1b,
qwen3-asr-1.7b) on 15 English NPR-style meeting clips (avg 38 min each,
572 min total, 95302 reference words). 30-second chunking via
`tests/sugr_bench_top3.py` (new driver).

**Results:**

| Model | Avg WER | Min | Max | Avg RTF |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **qwen3-asr-1.7b** | **4.64%** | 2.69% | 18.23% | 0.325 |
| parakeet-tdt-1.1b | 5.28% | 3.01% | 13.56% | **0.026** |
| cohere-transcribe-2b | 25.62% | 10.98% | 63.10% | 0.060 |

**Findings:**

- **Qwen3-ASR-1.7B is the long-form accuracy leader** (wins 14 of 15 clips
  head-to-head vs Parakeet). Only en_12 — the shortest, noisiest clip —
  goes to Parakeet.
- **Parakeet is ~12.5× faster** than Qwen3 in every regime tested.
  RTF 0.026 = 38× real-time vs Qwen3's 0.325 = 3× real-time. Same
  accuracy regime within 0.64 pp.
- **Cohere collapses on long-form.** 25.62% avg WER vs 1.80% on LS-clean
  — a 14× degradation. Root cause: chunk-level EOS truncation. Cohere's
  hypothesis contains only 38-93% of reference word count per clip,
  whereas Qwen3/Parakeet both match within ±2%. The Cohere decoder emits
  early EOS on silence/music/speaker-transition chunk boundaries.
- **Short-form benchmarks do not predict long-form performance.** This
  is the headline takeaway. LS-clean rankings (Cohere ≈ Parakeet ≈ Qwen3
  at ~1.8%) invert dramatically on real meeting content.

### 3. Cohere runner bug (found and fixed)

The first sugr run produced WER=100% on all 15 Cohere clips. Root cause:

```python
# old
text = self.processor.decode(outputs, skip_special_tokens=True)
```

`processor.decode()` on a 2D `[batch, seq]` tensor returns a Python list.
`str(list)` produces `'[" text..."]'`. The whisper normalizer collapses
that list-repr wrapper to an empty string, hence WER=100% on all clips
despite real content being produced.

**Fix** (`runners/cohere_runner.py`):

```python
# new
if hasattr(self.processor, "batch_decode"):
    texts = self.processor.batch_decode(outputs, skip_special_tokens=True)
    text = texts[0] if texts else ""
else:
    decoded = self.processor.decode(outputs, skip_special_tokens=True)
    text = decoded[0] if isinstance(decoded, list) else decoded
return text.strip()
```

Verified fix: first chunk of clip en_1 returns a clean 415-char string
with full transcribed content. Re-run gave the 25.62% number above.

**The earlier LibriSpeech Cohere result (1.80%) was not affected** —
different decode path (full-utterance decode, not chunked), confirmed by
inspecting cached transcripts.

### 4. Qwen3-ASR-1.7B deployment to Orin

Model not cached on Jetson; HF direct and hf-mirror.com both failed from
Orin (connection resets on large transfers). Workaround: downloaded on
Mac using `HF_HUB_ENABLE_HF_TRANSFER=1`, then `rsync` to
`x@100.123.48.6:/home/x/.cache/huggingface/hub/`. Ran inference offline
with `TRANSFORMERS_OFFLINE=1 HF_HUB_OFFLINE=1`. 4.6 GB model loaded in
19.6 s on Orin.

Side effect: installing `qwen-asr` pip package downgraded transformers
from 5.4.0 → 4.57.6 in vibevoice-venv. Cohere runner requires ≥5.5.0 per
its own docstring; re-running Cohere in vibevoice-venv will now fail.
Both Cohere and Parakeet results are banked so no immediate impact, but
future Cohere work needs a dedicated env.

## Deployment recommendations (updated in RESULTS.md)

- **Bosco English-only, streaming on Orin:** `parakeet-tdt-1.1b`. 5.28% WER,
  RTF 0.026 — leaves plenty of budget for LLM summarizer + PII washer.
- **Bosco English-only, overnight batch:** `qwen3-asr-1.7b`. +0.64 pp
  accuracy at 12× slowdown, worth it when wall-clock doesn't matter.
- **Bosco multilingual (EN/ZH/JA):** `cohere-transcribe-2b` remains the
  only viable top-tier single model. Mitigate long-form chunk truncation
  with larger chunks or alternative chunking strategy.
- **Bosco multilingual (EN/ZH/JA) if willing to route per-language:**
  `parakeet-tdt-1.1b` (EN) + `qwen3-asr-1.7b` (ZH, 1.57% CER) +
  `moonshine-base-ja` (JA, 7.11% CER). Lowest total VRAM, best per-language
  accuracy, but three models to orchestrate.

## Language coverage reference

- **parakeet-tdt-1.1b**: English only.
- **parakeet-tdt-0.6b-v3**: 25 European languages, no ZH/JA/KO/AR.
- **qwen3-asr-1.7b**: 30 languages + 22 Chinese dialects, covers EN/ZH/JA/KO
  plus Wu (Shanghainese), Minnan, Cantonese (HK + GD accents).
- **cohere-transcribe-2b**: 14 languages incl. EN/ZH/JA/KO/AR + 9 European.
- **moonshine-base-{en,ja,zh}**: three separate monolingual models.

## Speaker diarization recommendation

Parakeet has no built-in diarization. Three realistic options on Orin:

1. **NVIDIA Sortformer** (`nvidia/diar_sortformer_4spk-v1`) — same NeMo
   runtime as Parakeet, ~1.5 GB VRAM. Best integration, caps at 4 speakers.
2. **pyannote 3.1** (`pyannote/speaker-diarization-3.1`) — battle-tested,
   best accuracy on meeting data, handles unknown speaker count. ~2 GB VRAM.
   Offline only.
3. **Diart** — streaming pyannote variant, real-time diarization for live
   meetings.

**Default:** pyannote 3.1 + Parakeet for Bosco offline (proven combo, shipped
in WhisperX and Riva reference pipeline). Sortformer + Parakeet for live /
real-time use where everything must stay in one NeMo runtime.

## Final ASR model selection for Bosco on Jetson Orin 32 GB

### Per-language winners

**English — Parakeet-TDT-1.1B (day) / Qwen3-ASR-1.7B (night)**

Parakeet is the pragmatic EN choice: 5.28% long-form WER at RTF 0.026
(38× real-time), leaves 28 GB for LLM + washer. The 0.64 pp gap to
Qwen3's 4.64% doesn't justify the 12× slowdown for streaming. For
overnight batch re-transcription (BoscoV4 night job), swap to Qwen3 for
the accuracy edge when wall-clock doesn't matter.

**Chinese — Qwen3-ASR-1.7B (unanimous)**

1.57% CER on AISHELL-1 — beats Qwen3-ASR-0.6B (2.13%), Cohere (4.21%),
and Moonshine-zh (6.88%) by wide margins. Only model that covers 22
Chinese regional dialects (Cantonese, Wu/Shanghainese, Minnan/Hokkien,
plus 19 provincial accents). No other model in the bench supports these.
Use for both day and night.

**Japanese — Moonshine-base-ja (surprising winner)**

7.11% CER on ReazonSpeech, beating Cohere (9.70%) and every other
multi-billion-param model. Does it with 61M params and 153 MB VRAM —
27× less VRAM than Cohere, 33× fewer params. A specialized tiny model
crushing generalists. Qwen3-ASR is terrible on JA (42.02% CER) despite
claiming 30-language support — unusable.

### Day mode (all 3 loaded concurrently)

```
Parakeet-TDT-1.1B (EN)  — 4.2 GB VRAM
Qwen3-ASR-1.7B (ZH)     — 4.2 GB VRAM
Moonshine-base-ja (JA)   — 0.15 GB VRAM
                           ─────────────
Total                    — 8.6 GB VRAM
Remaining for LLM        — 23.4 GB
```

Route by language detection on first ~2s of audio. All three models run
faster than real-time. Parakeet at 38× RT handles English streaming
effortlessly. Qwen3 at 3× RT keeps up for Chinese meetings. Moonshine-ja
at ~6× RT is more than sufficient.

### Night mode (batch re-transcription)

```
Qwen3-ASR-1.7B (EN + ZH) — 4.2 GB VRAM
Moonshine-base-ja (JA)    — 0.15 GB VRAM
                            ─────────────
Total                     — 4.4 GB VRAM
Remaining for night LLM   — 27.6 GB
```

Qwen3 handles both EN (4.64% WER) and ZH (1.57% CER) at night. Only
one model to load for two languages. Swap Moonshine-ja for JA segments.
Maximum VRAM headroom for the 122B night LLM (Qwen3.5-122B-A10B at
~28 GB with SSD offload).

### Open validation gap

Qwen3-ASR on long-form Chinese meetings is untested. Our 4.64% WER is
EN-only (sugr bench). AISHELL-1 is short-utterance Mandarin. Real-world
Chinese meeting audio with code-switching, dialects, and overlapping
speakers may behave differently. Worth queuing a Chinese sugr-style test
if Chinese meeting recordings are available.
