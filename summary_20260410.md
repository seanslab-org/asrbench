**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Author:** Sean Song + Claude Opus 4.6

**Hardware:** Jetson AGX Orin 32GB, iPad mini 6 (A15), Mac mini (Apple Silicon), MacBook Air M2

---

## 1. Project goal

Select the best ASR model for three downstream projects:
- **Bosco** — LAN meeting transcription on Jetson Orin (EN/ZH/JA)
- **lonote** — home AI meeting system on Jetson Orin (EN/ZH/JA)
- **Memio** — pocket recording device, now targeting iPad (EN primary)

Criteria: WER/CER accuracy, inference speed (RTF), VRAM/RAM footprint,
multilingual coverage, hallucination safety, and on-device viability.

## 3. Platform-specific results

### 3.1 Jetson AGX Orin 32GB (JetPack 6, CUDA 12.6, PyTorch 2.8)

Primary bench platform. All models benchmarked here on standard datasets
(LibriSpeech test-clean 2620, AISHELL-1 7176, ReazonSpeech 500).

**Moonshine Flavors (transformers, GPU fp16):**
- 6 runners (tiny/base × en/ja/zh), 4114 total records, 0 errors, 0 hallucinations
- moonshine-base-ja **7.11% CER** = new JA leader (beats Cohere 9.70%, Whisper 26.92%)
- moonshine-base-zh **6.88% CER** (1k subset) — beats Whisper (9.31%), trails Cohere/Qwen
- moonshine-base-en **4.54% WER** (full set) — competitive but not leader

**Moonshine ONNX (CPU, legacy useful-moonshine-onnx):**
- 388-sample subset: base 2.55% WER at 17× RT, tiny 3.48% WER at 28× RT
- Zero VRAM, ~330–585 MB RAM

**VibeVoice-ASR (transformers, BF16):**
- 386-sample subset: 3.11% WER, 16.4 GB VRAM, RTF 0.84 (barely real-time)
- Only model with built-in speaker diarization
- Load time 7 min from NTFS SSD, required `attn_implementation="eager"` workaround
- ZH/JA not benchmarked

### 3.2 Jetson Orin 16GB (JetPack 5, earlier bench)

Constrained platform. Key limitations:
- Python 3.8 lock-in blocks Moonshine (needs 3.9+) and FunASR
- CTranslate2 lacks CUDA on aarch64 → faster-whisper falls back to CPU
- whisper-large-v3 OOMs (only turbo fits)

### 3.3 iPad mini 6 (A15 Bionic, iPadOS 26.3)

First-ever Moonshine vs Apple Speech head-to-head on real Apple hardware.

| Runner | WER% | RTF | Latency |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **moonshine-base-en** | **2.45** | 0.053 (19× RT) | 0.396s |
| **moonshine-tiny-en** | **4.69** | 0.036 (28× RT) | 0.267s |
| apple-speech-en-US | **10.92** | 0.038 (26× RT) | 0.280s |

**Moonshine-base beats Apple Speech by 4.5× on accuracy at the same speed.**
50 samples, 0 errors, 150 total records.

### 3.4 Mac mini (iOS Simulator, Mac CPU)

Validated moonshine-swift xcframework correctness (2.55% WER = matches ONNX
baseline). Apple Speech impossible on simulator (`AssetInventory.status ==
.unsupported` on new API; `kLSRErrorDomain Code=300` on legacy API).

## 4. Key discoveries and surprises

### 4.1 Moonshine went multilingual (the headline finding)

The asrbench project started with Moonshine marked "EN only." The Sep 2025
"Flavors of Moonshine" release (arXiv 2509.02523) changed this — separate
monolingual models for AR/ZH/JA/KO/UK/VI. The JA model at **7.11% CER beats
everything else** including models 33× larger. This single discovery changed
the lonote/Memio ASR recommendation.

### 4.2 Whisper hallucinates on silence

All three tested non-speech inputs (silence, white noise, HVAC hum) produced
"Thank you." from Whisper-turbo. Qwen3-ASR correctly outputs nothing. This is
a critical safety issue for meeting transcription — Whisper generates phantom
text during pauses.

### 4.3 Apple Speech is surprisingly inaccurate

Apple's native `SFSpeechRecognizer` delivered **10.92% WER** on the same audio
where Moonshine-base got 2.45%. Apple makes real word-level errors ("Stuffed
into you" vs correct "Stuff it into you"). Apple's new iOS 26 SpeechTranscriber
API crashes on iPad mini 6 (SIGTRAP — not in hardware allowlist).

### 4.4 VibeVoice is the only model with built-in diarization

At 16.4 GB VRAM and RTF 0.84, it's heavy and slow — but it's the only model
that identifies speakers without a separate pyannote step. The accuracy
(3.11% WER) is solid. Community INT8 (~12 GB) and INT4 (~6.6 GB) quantizations
exist but ASR-specific WER impact is unverified.

### 4.5 Subset vs full-set WER gap is real

moonshine-base-en: 2.55% on 388-sample subset, 4.54% on full 2620. The
~1.8× difference comes from speaker diversity (subset is single-speaker;
full set has many accents). Always note the sample count when comparing.

## 5. Technical lessons learned

### 5.1 Jetson deployment

1. **HuggingFace unreachable from Jetson** (GFW/DNS policy). Cache on Mac →
   rsync via LAN → run with `HF_HUB_OFFLINE=1`. hf-mirror.com works as alt.
2. **Jetson SSD is NTFS** (fuseblk) — no symlinks, `python -m venv` fails on
   SSD. Venv goes on root ext4 partition.
3. **PyTorch Jetson wheels**: use `pypi.jetson-ai-lab.io/jp6/cu126`, not the
   old nvidia redist URLs.
4. **xet download protocol** is unreliable — parallel curl from Mac is faster.
5. **Tailscale throughput** ~1–2 MB/s for large transfers — prefer LAN.
6. **Always auto-detect CUDA**: don't hardcode `device="cuda"`.

### 5.2 iOS development (headless Mac mini)

7. **Keychain must be unlocked before every build**: `security unlock-keychain`
   + `security set-key-partition-list -S apple-tool:,apple:` for codesign.
8. **`xcrun simctl privacy grant speech-recognition`** doesn't work on
   Xcode 26 — must insert directly into simulator's TCC.db.
9. **iOS Simulator doesn't ship Speech model assets** — confirmed by
   `AssetInventory.status == .unsupported` (new API) and `kLSRErrorDomain
   Code=300` (legacy API). Not fixable.
10. **iOS 26 SpeechTranscriber crashes on iPad mini 6** with SIGTRAP — Apple
    hasn't added A15 to their hardware allowlist. Legacy SFSpeechRecognizer
    works fine.
11. **xcodegen resources**: use `sources:` entries with `buildPhase: resources`
    for folder references — the top-level `resources:` stanza doesn't bundle.
12. **Free dev cert trust**: after each uninstall/reinstall, user must re-trust
    the developer cert in Settings → General → VPN & Device Management.
13. **iPad auto-lock suspends async tasks** — keep screen on during benchmarks.
    Background audio mode declared but iOS still suspends non-audio work.

### 5.3 Moonshine-specific

14. **Model card token-limit recipe**: `max_length = ceil(input_frames × 13 /
    16000)`. Confirmed not too aggressive — beam search and longer caps yield
    identical outputs. Floor at 8 tokens for very short clips.
15. **Three runtimes, identical accuracy**: Swift xcframework (2.45%), Python
    ONNX (2.55%), Python transformers (2.55% on same subset) — validates
    cross-platform consistency.
16. **moonshine-voice Python package**: `pip install moonshine-voice`, then
    `python -m moonshine_voice.download --language en --model-arch 1` (1=base).
    Model cache at `~/Library/Caches/moonshine_voice/`. Requires Python 3.10+
    (moonshine-voice uses `X | Y` union syntax).

### 5.4 Dataset handling

17. **ReazonSpeech pyarrow fast-path**: HF `datasets` Audio decoder pulls in
    torchcodec which fails on Jetson (`libnvrtc.so.13` missing). Fixed by
    reading the `transcription` column directly via pyarrow IPC without going
    through the Audio decoder. Pre-extracted WAVs in `test_wav/` directory.
18. **Verify audio clips before benchmarking**: bad clips from yt-dlp search
    (wrong content, silence, music) waste hours. Use specific verified URLs.
19. **Jetson rebooted mid-bench**: results saved to `results/<timestamp>/`
    before reboot, no data lost. `/tmp/` log was lost but JSON was on SSD.

## 6. Infrastructure built

### 6.1 asrbench framework

- `bench.py` — CLI with `standard` and `custom` modes, `--limit N` for
  sample-capped runs, per-model language filtering, hallucination detection
- `runners/` — pluggable runner registry: whisper, faster-whisper, qwen-asr,
  sensevoice, firered, parakeet, cohere, moonshine, vibevoice, voxtral,
  kotoba (11 runners total)
- `asrdatasets/` — LibriSpeech clean/other, AISHELL-1, ReazonSpeech with
  pyarrow fast-path
- `metrics/` — WER/CER with language-specific normalization, profiler with
  VRAM/RTF tracking

### 6.2 iOS subproject (asrbench/ios/)

- xcodegen-based Xcode project, moonshine-swift v0.0.51 dependency
- AppleSpeechRunner (SFSpeechRecognizer, fail-loud on-device), MoonshineRunner,
  BenchRunner orchestrator
- 50-sample LibriSpeech WAV bundle (16k mono PCM_16, 11.5 MB)
- `scripts/compute_metrics.py` reuses asrbench's normalization for
  apples-to-apples comparison
- `scripts/setup_mini.sh` for one-shot Mac mini setup

### 6.3 Mac mini dev environment (built from scratch)

Started with Command Line Tools only. Now has: Homebrew, Xcode 26.4, iOS 26.4
Simulator runtime, xcodegen, moonshine-voice in venv, Screen Sharing enabled,
signing cert from MacBook Air imported. Full CLI build+deploy+test pipeline
over SSH (no GUI needed except one-time Apple ID sign-in).

### 6.4 Memio iOS scaffold (Memio/ios/)

Phase 1 Record + Transcribe app: SwiftUI with PTT button, AVAudioEngine 16k
mono capture, MoonshineTranscriber batch transcription, MeetingStore (JSON),
HiNotesClient for server. Built, signed, installed, launched on iPad mini 6.

## 7. Deployment recommendations

### 7.1 For Jetson Orin 32GB (Bosco / lonote)

| Option | Models | VRAM | EN | ZH | JA | Best for |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| **A: Single** | Cohere 2B | 4.1 GB | 1.80% | 4.21% | 9.70% | Simplicity |
| **B: Routed** | Cohere + Qwen + Moonshine-ja | ~6 GB | 1.80% | 1.57% | 7.11% | Best per-lang |
| **C: Moonshine-only** | base × {en,ja,zh} | ~460 MB | 4.54% | 6.88% | 7.11% | Min VRAM |
| **D: Qwen single** | Qwen3-0.6B | 1.8 GB | 2.50% | 2.13% | 47.46% | No JA needed |
| **E: CPU only** | Moonshine ONNX | 0 | 2.55% | — | — | EN-only, free GPU |

### 7.2 For iPad / Memio

**Moonshine-base-en via moonshine-swift** — the only viable option.
- 2.45% WER, 19× real-time on A15
- ~585 MB RAM (fits in iPad's 4 GB)
- Beats Apple's own SFSpeechRecognizer by 4.5×
- Available for JA/ZH via `moonshine_voice.download --language ja|zh`
  (ONNX conversion needed for Swift package)
